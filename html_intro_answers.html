The Great Gatsby

A Classical novel written by F. Scott Fitzgerald.

Main Character

Jay Gatsby

Jay Gatsby is a wealthy and enigmatic man who is deeply in love with Diasy Buchanan.

Diasy Buchanan

Daisy Buchanan is Gatsby's former lover, now married to Tom Buchanan. She represents Gatsby's ultimate dream.

Nick Carraway

Nick Carraway, the narrator, provides an outsider's perpective on Gatsby's world of wealth and longing.

Plot Summarry

The novel follows Nick Carraway as he moves to West Egg, Long Island, and becomes entangled in the lavish world of Jay Gatsby, who throws extravagant parties in hopes of rekindling his romance with Daisy Buchanan.

Setting

West Egg

West Egg represents new wealth and extravagance, home to Jay Gatsby.

East Egg

East Egg is home to the old aristocracy, including Diasy and Tom Buchanan.

Personal Thoughts

The Great Gatsby is a timeless novel that explores themes of love, wealth, and the American Dream. Fitzgerald's prose beautifully captures the extravagance and emptiness of the Roaring Twenties.
